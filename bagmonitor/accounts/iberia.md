## Context

- **Group:** IAG (International Airlines Group) — includes British Airways, Aer Lingus, Level, Iberia
- **Handler:** "South" in Spain; varies by destination airport
- **Pain:** High indemnization costs + NPS damage from delays (more than total losses); seasonal spikes (August volume)
- **Confidentiality:** Hard data (volume, indemnizations) requires NDA before sharing
- **Prior PoC:** Tested BLE tracking with startup "Bob" — failed due to airport connectivity gaps and blind spots

---

## Key Objections

1. **Prior failed PoC** — Already tried BLE tags (startup "Bob"), failed due to fixed antenna coverage gaps. BagMonitor's smartphone-as-node model directly addresses this, but needs to be demonstrated clearly.
2. **Operational overhead** — Any process that adds seconds to handlers or crew is a non-starter at scale.
3. **Decentralized handlers** — Iberia uses different handlers at each airport globally; rollout complexity is real.

## Entry Path

**IAGI Accelerator** — annual call, run by IAG's innovation arm. Presenting there gives visibility to all IAG airlines simultaneously (BA, Aer Lingus, Level, Iberia). Carolina explicitly recommended this as the fastest and most direct route.

Carolina's team (Innovation) acts as the bridge between startups and Iberia's Baggage operations department. Direct outreach to Baggage is difficult without this bridge.
