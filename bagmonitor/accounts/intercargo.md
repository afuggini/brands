## Context

- **Role:** Argentina's main ground handler — loads/unloads baggage for most airlines at Ezeiza and Aeroparque
- **Current state:** State-owned, undergoing privatization process. No capital expenditure approved
- **Pain:** Theft and content tampering during handling — unable to determine if it occurs at origin or at Intercargo
- **Strategic relevance:** Intercargo physically handles baggage for all airlines. A partnership would give BagMonitor ramp-level coverage without needing to sell each airline separately
- **Note from Lufthansa transcript:** Diego (Lufthansa) described "robo hormiga de Intercargo en la rampa" — systematic petty theft by Intercargo staff. Intercargo itself doesn't know where in the chain theft occurs

---

## Key Objections

1. **Privatization freeze**: No budget decisions until licitación is resolved
2. **Not a buyer**: Intercargo doesn't pay for the solution — partnership model only

---

## Partnership Model

Intercargo provides ramp staff who use the BagMonitor app during loading/unloading. Airlines get full coverage data. Intercargo gains liability evidence (can prove where/when theft occurred — or didn't). Zero cost to Intercargo, differentiating value-add to their airline clients.
