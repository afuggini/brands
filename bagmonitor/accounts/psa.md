## Context

- **Role:** PSA is the federal airport security police. Controls baggage inspection and BHS oversight at Ezeiza
- **BHS system:** Thousands of trays. 3 reading points: (1) pre-tomograph arc, (2) bag sorter (determines carousel), (3) reinspection re-entry
- **Belt speed:** ~0.5 m/s on most segments; faster on non-reading segments
- **No-read metric:** Currently stable at 3% (down from 4.6% after BHS programming adjustments). Gabriel monitors this monthly. BagMonitor would address this floor
- **Infrastructure alternative being displaced:** AA2000 and Vanderlande proposed breaking concrete to add recirculation belt + new tomograph + new reading arc. BagMonitor tag = same outcome without construction
- **PSA primary use case:** Redundancy tracking — if a bag loses BHS tracking, tag provides re-link without physical search
- **PSA secondary use case:** Security/investigation — double tracking loss (bag leaves tray at unexpected point) triggers alert with location + access log → identifies theft or tampering incidents
- **Current PSA priority blocking progress:** Importing an algorithm update for explosive detection (discarding false positives from toy grenades, souvenir cartridges). This is their #1 priority; BagMonitor is #2
- **Vanderlande contact:** Ariel has a contact at Vanderlande (via Pablo Ferrari) who validated the no-read problem and offered to advise on the BHS in person
