# BagMonitor — Writing Rules

Operational rules for writing BagMonitor copy. These apply on top of BRAND.md voice guidelines.

---

## Scan-first density

Users on landing pages don't read — they scan. Every line must carry weight on its own.

- **Avoid empty words.** Cut qualifiers, hedging modals ("can", "may", "might"), and filler phrases. If removing a word leaves the sentence intact, cut it.
- **Lead with facts.** Numbers, data points, and named mechanisms over generalities. "~$150 per event" > "significant operational costs."
- **One idea per line.** In feature blocks and cost breakdowns, each line should be readable in isolation.
- **Cut editorial.** Avoid commentary disguised as description ("the cost of a bag whose location should already be known"). Reserve editorial for headlines and intro hooks, never for factual blocks.
