## goal
The reader finishes understanding that they — the airport operator — are the right buyer for end-to-end baggage visibility, that they already have the commercial lever to deploy it terminal-wide without airline-by-airline negotiation, and that attaching to infrastructure they're already purchasing (biometric bag drop) makes the investment case immediate. BagMonitor is introduced as the mechanism at the close.

## reader
Airport commercial director, COO, or innovation leader who is already investing in or evaluating unified terminal intelligence platforms — but hasn't framed baggage as the missing layer. They've seen the airline-by-airline coordination problem as an obstacle and quietly concluded that terminal-wide baggage visibility is too complicated to pursue.

## angle
The reason baggage visibility has stayed fragmented across terminals isn't technology — it's procurement sequencing. Airlines are the wrong first buyer. The airport operator already has the commercial relationship that makes terminal-wide deployment a single decision, not twelve.

## structure
1. Lede — the missing layer: Unified terminal intelligence is a recognized capital investment category (Heathrow £1.3B, Delhi UTAM, Changi). Power, HVAC, gates, security — all have real-time visibility layers. Baggage handling, the process most directly tied to airline retention, runs without one.
2. Why baggage stayed blind: Baggage visibility has been treated as each airline's individual problem. That framing produces a 12-vendor coordination puzzle for infrastructure that operates in the same building, under the same roof the airport operator runs.
3. The procurement reframe: The airport operator already has a commercial relationship with every airline in the terminal. Deploying visibility once at the terminal level — and extending access through that relationship — collapses the coordination problem into a single decision.
4. The entry point that already exists: Biometric bag drop is on track to go from 19% to 65% of airports by 2027. Airport operators are the buyers. The acceptance event at a biometric drop is the natural first link in a continuous tracking chain. BagMonitor attaches here — to infrastructure already being purchased, not a net-new capital line.
5. Close — BagMonitor: One deployment, terminal-wide visibility, every airline in the building gets access. What that looks like in practice + Book a Demo CTA.

## hook-type
How-to promise (14) — the title carries the frame; the lede delivers on it immediately by establishing the unified terminal intelligence trend as the concrete, credible starting point.
