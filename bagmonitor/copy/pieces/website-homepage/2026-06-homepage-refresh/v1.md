## The Problem

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a **visibility failure.**

**Subheading:**
Every mishandled bag had a scan record at check-in. Between that moment and the aircraft, data handoffs break down — at transfers, loading zones, and carrier handovers. The visibility gap is structural.
*(→ reescrita: la frase anterior "invisible at the moment it failed" era ambigua; la nueva es declarativa, no usa el patrón negación/afirmación, y nombra los puntos concretos de falla)*

**Stats:**

| Stat | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 33.4M | bags mishandled in 2024 | Global air traffic hit 5.3 billion passengers in 2024. Baggage volumes scale with it — but real-time visibility infrastructure has not. *(→ c0desc actualizada: el dato de crecimiento, no el mecanismo técnico)* |
| **58%** | of delayed bags fail in the gaps | **41% at transfer handoffs. 17% on the ramp.** The two biggest failure points share one root cause: no visibility between mandatory scan checkpoints. *(→ era "41% of delayed bags lost at transfer" — 58% es el número completo del gap que BagMonitor cubre)* |
| $5B | annual industry cost | Spent on compensation, tracing, courier returns, and storage — across an industry that can measure the problem but hasn't solved it. *(sin cambios)* |
| 74% | of mishandled bags are just delayed | Not destroyed. Not stolen. Simply invisible at the wrong moment — a visibility failure, not a loss. *(sin cambios)* |
