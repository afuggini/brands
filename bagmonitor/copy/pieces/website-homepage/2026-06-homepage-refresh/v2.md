## The Problem

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a **visibility failure.**

**Subheading:**
The $5B annual cost of mishandled baggage is the industry's accepted price for a preventable problem. Each event costs at least $150 in operational recovery. The data needed to prevent it existed — it stopped flowing somewhere between checkpoints.

**Stats:**

| Stat | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 33.4M | bags mishandled in 2024 | At $150 per event in confirmed operational cost, that's a recoverable liability of over $5 billion — before WorldTracer fees and passenger compensation. |
| 58% | of delayed bags fail in the gaps | 41% at transfer handoffs. 17% on the ramp. Both failure types share the same root: no continuous data between mandatory scan checkpoints. |
| $150 | operational cost per mishandled bag | The confirmed floor: logistics, staff time, and claims handling. WorldTracer adds $22 when the bag enters active tracing. Passenger compensation is a separate, additional layer. |
| 74% | of mishandled bags are just delayed | Delayed — and invisible. Most of those bags could have been recovered in real time with a single alert at the point of failure. |
