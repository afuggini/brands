# The Cost of No Visibility — v1

**Eyebrow:** The Cost

**Heading:** One mishandled bag. Three cost layers.

**Intro:**
A single mishandling event generates three separate, compounding costs. Most internal estimates capture only the first. The combined exposure — operational, tracing, and compensation — is what the industry spends $5 billion a year on.

---

**Operational cost**
- ~$150 per event: courier transport, staff hours, claims processing overhead (IATA reference)
- Confirmed in the field: Sky Airlines (Hub Ops, Santiago/Lima), Aegean Airlines (Head of Ground Ops, Athens) — both use $150 as their working baseline
- Applies to every mishandling event, before tracing or compensation is considered
- This is the cost that activates before anyone knows the bag is delayed

**Tracing cost**
- $22 per WorldTracer activation — the fee for locating a bag whose position is unknown
- Conditional: applies only when the airline cannot account for the bag's location; not every event triggers it
- WorldTracer activates after the bag is already missing — it is a recovery cost, not a prevention cost
- Manual agent time, call center volume, and courier dispatches compound the per-event overhead

**Compensation exposure**
- Montreal Convention (international): up to ~$2,091 per passenger (1,519 SDRs, effective December 28, 2024)
- US domestic: $4,700 minimum per passenger (14 CFR §254.4, effective January 22, 2025) — a floor, not a ceiling
- The US domestic minimum is 2.3× the international ceiling — an airline that loses a bag on a domestic leg faces higher exposure than on a transoceanic one
- 74% of mishandled bags are delays, not permanent losses — each still triggers a full claims workflow

**Interline penalty**
- IATA Resolution 753: a carrier that cannot prove custody through tracking data absorbs 100% of interline mishandling costs, regardless of which carrier caused the failure
- The penalty applies to the carrier with the least data — not the carrier at fault
- R753 mandates tracking at four points; 58% of failures occur between them
- No data at the handoff = no ability to contest cost allocation
