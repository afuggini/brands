# The Cost of No Visibility — v2

**Eyebrow:** The Cost

**Heading:** $5 billion a year. 58% of it at two predictable points.

**Intro:**
The airline industry spends approximately $5 billion annually on mishandled baggage — 33.4 million events in 2024 alone. The number looks diffuse. The failures are not: 41% of delayed bags fail at transfers, 17% fail to load. The cost concentrates exactly where the data doesn't.

---

**The aggregate**
- $5B annual industry cost of mishandled baggage (SITA 2025)
- 33.4 million bags mishandled in 2024 at 6.3 per 1,000 passengers
- The mishandling rate has improved 67% since 2007 — but the absolute number of mishandled bags has barely fallen, because passenger volumes grew +114% in the same period
- Global traffic is projected to reach 8 billion passengers by 2037 — the cost base will grow unless the infrastructure changes

**The per-event baseline**
- ~$150 per event: operational floor — courier, staff, claims overhead (IATA; Sky Airlines and Aegean Airlines field-confirmed)
- +$22 WorldTracer fee per activation (conditional — applies when bag location is unknown)
- Compensation: up to ~$2,091 per passenger internationally (Montreal Convention, 1,519 SDRs); up to $4,700 on US domestic routes (14 CFR §254.4)
- Full exposure per claimed event: $150 confirmed + $22 conditional + jurisdiction-dependent compensation

**The concentration**
- 41% of delayed bags: transfer mishandling (SITA 2025)
- 17% of delayed bags: failure to load (SITA 2025)
- 58% of all failures occur at two addressable, predictable points — both between the four mandatory IATA R753 scan points
- International routes run at 11.2 per 1,000 passengers; domestic at 1.9 — nearly 6× the rate on the routes with the highest transfer complexity

**The compounding effect**
- 74% of mishandled bags are delays, not permanent losses — each generates the same claims and tracing overhead as a lost bag
- Iberia (Innovation Dept.): "a veces un retraso te lo terminan reclamando como pérdida" — delay events escalate to loss claims at a measurable rate
- On interline itineraries, the carrier without custody data absorbs 100% of the cost regardless of fault (IATA R753)
- The $5B is the cost of the system as it exists. It is not the cost of a problem that can't be solved.
