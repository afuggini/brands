# The Cost of No Visibility — v3

**Eyebrow:** The Cost

**Heading:** Without data, someone pays. Without visibility, that someone is always you.

**Intro:**
A mishandling event generates costs across three layers — operational, tracing, and compensation. Which carrier absorbs them on interline segments is not determined by fault. It is determined by who has the data. The carrier with the least documentation absorbs the most.

---

**Operational overhead**
- ~$150 per mishandling event: courier transport, staff hours, claims overhead (IATA reference)
- Confirmed in the field: Sky Airlines (Santiago/Lima hub ops), Aegean Airlines (Athens ground ops) — €100–$150 cited as the working per-event baseline by both
- This layer activates before the failure is even detected — and before compensation enters the calculation
- Sky Airlines (Arnold, International Airports): "El volumen de equipaje extraviado impacta económicamente por las compensaciones y daña muchísimo nuestro NPS" — compensation named as a separate, additional economic layer on top of the $150

**Compensation liability**
- Montreal Convention: up to ~$2,091 per passenger on international routes (1,519 SDRs; 18% increase, effective December 28, 2024)
- US domestic: $4,700 per passenger minimum — a floor with no ceiling on proven damages (14 CFR §254.4, effective January 22, 2025)
- The US domestic floor is now 2.3× the international ceiling — a transborder itinerary creates different exposure depending on which leg the failure occurs on
- Iberia (Carolina, Innovation): "Lo que más impacta es la pérdida total, pero lo que sucede en la gran mayoría de los casos es el retraso — y a veces un retraso te lo terminan reclamando como pérdida"

**Interline accountability**
- IATA Resolution 753: a carrier that cannot demonstrate custody through tracking data bears 100% of mishandling costs on interline segments — regardless of which carrier caused the failure
- R753 mandates tracking at four mandatory points; 58% of mishandling events occur between them
- No data at the transfer point = no defense against full cost absorption
- R753 creates zero passenger compensation obligations — its only financial mechanism is this inter-airline proration penalty

**The cost of not knowing**
- 74% of mishandled bags are delays, not losses — but each triggers the same operational, tracing, and claims overhead
- WorldTracer: $22 per activation — the cost of searching for a bag whose location is unknown; it activates after the bag is already missing
- $5B annually (SITA 2025) is what the industry spends in the absence of continuous visibility
- The cost does not go away when visibility is absent — it is redistributed to whoever has the least data
