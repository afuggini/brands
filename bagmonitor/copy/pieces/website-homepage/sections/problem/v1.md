# The Problem — v1

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
Every mishandled bag had a scan record at check-in. Between that moment and the aircraft, data handoffs break down — at transfers, loading zones, and carrier handovers. The visibility gap is structural.

---

**Stats:**

| Stat | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 33.4M | bags mishandled in 2024 | Global air traffic hit 5.3 billion passengers in 2024. Baggage volumes scale with it — but real-time visibility infrastructure has not. |
| 58% | of delayed bags fail in the gaps | 41% at transfer handoffs. 17% on the ramp. The two biggest failure points share one root cause: no visibility between mandatory scan checkpoints. |
| $5B | annual industry cost | Spent on compensation, tracing, courier returns, and storage — across an industry that can measure the problem but hasn't solved it. Source: SITA 2025. |
| 74% | of mishandled bags are just delayed | Not destroyed. Not stolen. Simply invisible at the wrong moment — a visibility failure, not a loss. |
