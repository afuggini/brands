# The Problem — v10

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
The industry logs four mandatory checkpoints per bag. What happens between them — at transfer, on the ramp, through secondary inspection — is invisible. That's where failures concentrate.

---

**The last system record and the aircraft are not the same thing**
BCM is sent at check-in. BPM should confirm the bag completed the BHS process — but it isn't reliably transmitted. Between the classification record and the aircraft hold, no automatic confirmation exists. A bag can complete every logged step and still miss the flight.

**Transfers are the largest failure point — and the least monitored**
Carrier handoffs, terminal changes, tight connection windows. 41% of delayed bags fail here. No real-time signal exists between leg one's landing and leg two's departure. By the time operations is alerted, the second flight has already left.

**A timestamp is not a location**
Compliance records the checkpoints. Operations needs to know what's happening now. When a passenger calls from baggage claim, the agent's answer is when the bag was last scanned — not where it is. The gap between those two things is a WorldTracer case, a courier dispatch, and a compensation event.

**Without data, accountability defaults to cost**
On interline itineraries, a carrier that cannot prove custody at each handoff absorbs 100% of the mishandling cost under IATA R753 — regardless of fault. Cargo theft has no recoverable origin point. Claims are paid without answers because there are no answers available.
