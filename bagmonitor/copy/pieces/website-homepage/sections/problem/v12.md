# The Problem — v12

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
The gaps aren't random. They occur at the same points, on every flight, every day — and the industry has no real-time signal for any of them.

---

**Ramp gap**
- Bags show as classified before loading is confirmed
- No automatic signal from BHS output to aircraft hold
- Ground crew reconcile from paper lists
- A bag that misses the flight generates no alert at departure

**Transfer gap**
- 41% of delayed bags fail at transfer points
- No real-time signal between connecting carrier handoffs
- Operations learns of failures after the connecting flight departs
- Tight connection windows leave no margin for manual verification

**Inspection detour**
- Bags pulled for secondary inspection drop out of the tracking chain
- No reentry signal when a bag is returned — or not returned — before departure
- No alert fires if an inspected bag misses its flight

**Interline void**
- Carrier handoffs happen outside a shared data layer
- No continuous custody record across operating carriers
- Liability defaults to the carrier with the least documentation
