# The Problem — v13

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
$5 billion per year in mishandling costs — and most of it is spent after the bag is already lost.

---

**Handling cost**
- 33.4 million bags mishandled globally in 2024 (SITA)
- ~$150 direct operational cost per mishandling event
- Manual tracing, courier dispatches, storage — incurred before the failure is even detected
- Cost compounds when failures cluster at peak operations

**Compensation risk**
- Montreal Convention liability cap: ~$2,091 per passenger (international)
- US domestic exposure: up to $4,700 per passenger
- 74% of mishandled bags are delayed, not lost — each still triggers a full claims workflow
- Claims are processed without data on where the failure occurred

**Tracing overhead**
- WorldTracer fee: $22 per active trace (Turkish, British Airways field data)
- 66% of mishandled bags return within 48h — after the tracing machinery is already running
- Call center volume, agent time, and passenger follow-up multiply cost per event

**Liability exposure**
- IATA R753 requires custody proof at four mandatory scan points
- Carriers without gap data absorb 100% of interline mishandling cost regardless of fault
- No data at the point of failure = no ability to contest liability or apportion cost
