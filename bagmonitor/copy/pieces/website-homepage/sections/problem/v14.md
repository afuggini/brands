# The Problem — v14

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
IATA mandates four scan points per bag. What happens between them — at transfer, on the ramp, through secondary inspection — is unmonitored by design.

---

**No ramp signal**
- No standard exists for continuous monitoring between BHS output and aircraft hold
- BPM transmission is inconsistent across airports and ground operators
- Between classification and wheels-up, the data model has a gap
- The industry filled it with paper reconciliation

**No transfer data**
- Transfer and ramp loading — 58% of mishandling — fall between the four mandatory scan points
- No real-time data exchange between carriers at transfer points
- IATA R753 compliance covers the checkpoints; the handoffs are outside it

**Compliance ≠ visibility**
- Compliance scanning records discrete events; it doesn't track real-time state
- A bag can clear every mandatory checkpoint and still miss the flight
- Operations has timestamps — not current location
- The gap between last scan and destination is where failures live

**No custody chain**
- Each operating carrier maintains its own siloed tracking record
- No shared data layer exists across carriers on interline itineraries
- When a bag goes missing, no single system has visibility of the full journey
- Liability disputes happen in the absence of data, not in spite of it
