# The Problem — v15

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
Airlines know when a bag enters the system. They don't know if it makes the flight. Everything in between is a gap.

---

**BCM gap**
- Airlines receive a BCM (Baggage Check-in Message) when a bag is accepted at check-in
- A BPM (Baggage Processed Message) should confirm the bag completed the BHS process — it's not reliably transmitted
- Airlines know a bag entered the system; they have no automatic confirmation it reached the aircraft hold
- Classification is not the last step — a bag can complete classification and still miss the flight

**Carousel failure**
- Bags that complete classification loop on sorting carousels until ground crew collects them
- If crew doesn't collect in time, bags pile up — the carousel pit (fosa) takes them
- The aircraft departs; records show the bag was processed and ready
- No alert fires: the system logged success at classification, not at loading

**No-pic diversion**
- CT scanners occasionally fail to produce a usable image (no pic result)
- The bag is diverted to a secondary inspection area and processed manually
- It rejoins the flow after manual clearance — with no automatic reentry signal in the tracking chain
- One scanner with a failing generator produces bursts of ~20 consecutive no-pic events

**Paper reconciliation**
- Between sorting carousels and aircraft loading, reconciliation is done with handheld scanners and paper bingo-style sheets
- Both require human intervention at every point
- Aerolíneas Argentinas confirmed use of paper lists as of May 2026
- This is the gap between the last system record and the aircraft hold
