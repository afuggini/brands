# The Problem — v16

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
Every mishandled bag generates three separate cost layers. Most airlines track only the first one.

---

**Operational cost**
- ~$150 per mishandling event: courier transportation, staff hours, claims processing overhead (IATA estimate; Sky Airlines confirmed)
- The $150 is the floor — it applies to every event before compensation is considered
- The industry's ~$5B annual figure bundles operational and compensation costs; SITA does not publish the breakdown

**WorldTracer fee**
- $22 per activation when a bag enters active tracing (location unknown)
- Conditional: does not apply to every event — only when the airline cannot already account for the bag's location
- WorldTracer is the recovery mechanism. It activates after the bag is already missing — not before

**Compensation exposure**
- Montreal Convention: 1,519 SDRs per passenger (~$2,091 USD) for international routes — effective December 28, 2024
- US domestic: $4,700 minimum floor per passenger (14 CFR § 254.4, effective January 22, 2025) — a floor, not a ceiling
- The US domestic floor is now 2.3× the international ceiling; an airline that loses a bag on a domestic leg faces higher exposure than on a transoceanic one
- 74% of mishandled bags are delays, not permanent losses — each still triggers a claims workflow

**Interline liability**
- IATA Resolution 753 mandates custody tracking at four points; the only financial mechanism is an inter-airline proration penalty
- A carrier that cannot demonstrate custody through tracking data bears 100% of mishandling costs on interline segments — regardless of which carrier caused the failure
- R753 creates zero passenger compensation obligations; passenger liability flows from the Montreal Convention
- No data at the point of failure = no ability to contest liability or apportion cost
