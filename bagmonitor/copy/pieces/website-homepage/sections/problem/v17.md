# The Problem — v17

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
The industry improved its mishandling rate by 67% since 2007. The absolute number of mishandled bags barely fell — because passenger volumes more than doubled.

---

**Volume growth**
- 5.3 billion passengers flew in 2024 — a record; +114% passenger growth since 2007
- 33.4 million bags mishandled in 2024 (6.3 per 1,000 passengers)
- Global traffic projected to reach 8 billion by 2037
- Rate improvement has not translated to fewer mishandled bags at scale

**Transfer concentration**
- 41% of delayed bags fail at transfer points (SITA 2025)
- 17% fail due to failure to load (SITA 2025)
- 58% of all delayed bags are lost at two predictable, addressable failure points
- Both fall between the four mandatory scan points required by IATA Resolution 753

**International gap**
- International mishandling rate: 11.2 per 1,000 passengers
- Domestic mishandling rate: 1.9 per 1,000 passengers
- International routes run nearly 6× higher than domestic — driven by connections, carrier handoffs, and tighter windows
- International improved only –7% year-on-year vs. –21% for domestic (SITA 2025)

**Tracking lag**
- 43% of airlines currently provide ops teams with real-time bag status; 92% plan to by 2027
- 42% currently offer passengers real-time tracking; 82% plan to by 2027
- The infrastructure to fulfill that commitment does not yet exist at scale
- IATA Modern Baggage Messaging (MBM) v2 was ratified in 2025 — designed to reduce mishandling by a further 5% through improved data standards
