# The Problem — v2

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
Each mishandling event generates costs across three separate layers: operational recovery, active tracing, and passenger compensation. Every layer is triggered after the failure. None of them are inevitable.

---

**Stats:**

| Stat | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| $150 | operational cost per mishandled bag | The confirmed floor per event: courier logistics, staff time, and claims handling. This cost applies to every mishandling event, every time. Source: IATA estimate; confirmed by field operators. |
| $22 | WorldTracer fee per active trace | The additional cost when a bag's location is unknown and enters active tracing. On top of the $150 — not instead of it. Source: industry standard, confirmed independently by multiple carriers. |
| Up to $4,700 | passenger compensation exposure | The minimum liability floor for US domestic routes under 14 CFR §254.4 (effective Jan 2025). On international routes, the Montreal Convention sets the cap at ~$2,091 USD per passenger. |
| $5B | annual industry cost | The combined total across all carriers globally — compensation, tracing, courier returns, and storage. Source: SITA 2025. Each line item in the stack above contributes to this number, event by event. |
