# The Problem — v3

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
International routes mishandle bags at 6 times the rate of domestic flights. The added complexity — tighter connections, multiple carrier handoffs, cross-border logistics — creates exactly the visibility gaps that continuous infrastructure is built to close.

---

**Stats:**

| Stat | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 6× | higher mishandling rate on international routes | 11.2 bags per 1,000 passengers on international routes vs. 1.9 on domestic. Every added carrier handoff, tight connection, and cross-border transfer compounds the visibility gap. Source: SITA 2025. |
| 41% | of delayed bags lost at transfer | The single largest failure point — and the most preventable. When a bag changes carriers or terminals, the data trail breaks. That break is where most mishandling originates. Source: SITA 2025. |
| $5B | annual industry cost | Compensation, tracing, courier returns, and storage — concentrated disproportionately on the international routes where visibility gaps are widest. Source: SITA 2025. |
| 8B | passengers projected by 2037 | Global traffic will grow 50% from today's 5.3 billion. International routes will absorb the largest share of that growth — and with it, the largest share of compounding operational pressure. Source: IATA. |
