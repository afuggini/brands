# The Problem — v9

**Eyebrow:** The Problem

**Heading:** Lost baggage is a visibility failure.

**Subheading:**
The visibility gap isn't the exception. It's the default state between every checkpoint, every handoff, and every flight.

---

**Before the door closes, you've already lost visibility**
Bags complete classification and show as ready before they're actually on the aircraft. Ground crew work from paper reconciliation lists. A bag diverted to secondary inspection drops out of the tracking chain with no automatic reentry signal. By the time the flight departs, there's no way to confirm what stayed behind.

**41% of failures happen at transfer**
Transfer is where carrier handoffs happen, terminals change, and tight connections run out. It's also where operational visibility breaks down most consistently. On connecting itineraries, there's no real-time signal telling your operations center whether a bag is on track or already behind — until it isn't.

**Your system logged it. Your agent can't explain it.**
A passenger calls from baggage claim. Their AirTag shows the bag is in Frankfurt. Your agent can tell them when the bag was last scanned — not where it is now. The records confirm the bag was processed. Between the last checkpoint and destination, there's no data and no answer to give.

**When there's a claim, there's no data**
High-value cargo goes missing from the ramp. Without location data, there's no addressable point in the chain where the failure occurred. On interline segments, if you can't prove custody at each handoff, you absorb 100% of the cost regardless of which carrier caused the failure.
