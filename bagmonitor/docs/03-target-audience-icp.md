# Target Audience & ICP

## Primary ICP

| Segment | Notes |
|---|---|
| Legacy airlines | Full-service carriers with complex hub operations |
| Low-cost carriers | High volume, cost-sensitive, operationally lean |
| Regional carriers | Smaller fleets, underserved by existing tools |
| International carriers | Complex multi-leg itineraries, high transfer risk |
| Ground handling companies | Direct operators of baggage workflows |
| Airport operators | Hub-and-spoke operators (e.g., Aeropuertos Argentina) can act as the primary buyer and distribute access to all airlines and security agencies operating in their terminals — removing multi-stakeholder coordination friction |

## Regional Context for Sales (From SITA 2025 Data)

Understanding regional performance gaps is important for tailoring the sales pitch by geography.

| Region | Mishandling Rate (2024) | Trend vs 2007 | Sales Angle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Europe | 12.3 per 1,000 pax | –26% | Still highest rate globally; significant room to improve |
| North America | 5.5 per 1,000 pax | –22% | Improved but high volume; IT investment narrative strong |
| Asia-Pacific | 3.1 per 1,000 pax | +1.6% | Lowest rate but facing manpower/infrastructure congestion |
| Global average | 6.3 per 1,000 pax | –67% since 2007 | Long-term trend positive but $5B annual cost persists |

**Key implication:** Europe is the highest-priority regional market for BagMonitor's cost-reduction narrative. Asia-Pacific is better approached through an operational scalability and congestion management angle given rapid volume growth.

## Airport Operator as Distribution Channel (LATAM Field Intelligence)

In the LATAM context, airport operators may function as a B2B distribution layer rather than just a secondary segment:

- The airport acquires BagMonitor and offers it as a terminal-wide infrastructure service
- Airlines receive access as part of their existing commercial relationship with the airport (analogous to how SITA messaging is reportedly bundled into check-in island fees at SEISA)
- The PSA/security agency benefits from identification capability without a separate procurement process
- Ground handlers operate within the infrastructure the airport deploys

This model collapses what would otherwise be a three-way coordination problem (airport + airlines + PSA) into a single enterprise sale. It is the recommended first-entry approach for LATAM, validated by field contact at Vanderlande (May 2026). Requires confirmation with Aeropuertos Argentina.
