# Market Intelligence

> This section contains third-party industry data to inform strategy, sales, and marketing. All figures from SITA 2025 Baggage IT Insights Report unless otherwise noted. This section should be updated annually as new SITA and IATA reports are released.

## The Scale of the Problem

- **5.3 billion passengers** flew in 2024 — a record, up 8.2% year-on-year
- **33.4 million bags** were mishandled in 2024
- **6.3 bags per 1,000 passengers** — the current global mishandling rate
- **$5 billion** — the annual cost to the airline industry of mishandled baggage
- Despite a 67% improvement in the mishandling rate since 2007, the absolute number of mishandled bags has barely fallen, because passenger volumes have more than doubled (+114% since 2007)
- Global passenger traffic is projected to reach **8 billion by 2037** — meaning the infrastructure gap will widen substantially without systematic investment

**BagMonitor implication:** The problem is not being solved fast enough. Volume growth is outpacing operational improvement. The industry needs infrastructure that scales.

## Root Causes of Baggage Mishandling

Understanding where bags actually fail is the foundation of BagMonitor's product narrative.

| Failure Type | Share of Delayed Bags | BagMonitor Response |
|---|---|---|
| Transfer mishandling | **41%** | Transfer monitoring, real-time handover alerts |
| Failure to load | **17%** | Scan-based gate validation, loading confirmation |
| Ticketing error / bag switch / security / other | 16% | Data reconciliation via API layer |
| Airport / customs / weather / weight constraints | 10% | Contextual exception flagging |
| Loading error | 8% | Scan verification at loading point |
| Arrival mishandling | 4% | Arrival confirmation tracking |
| Tagged error | 4% | Tag integrity verification |

**Key insight:** 58% of delayed bags (transfer mishandling + failure to load) are caused by operational failures at handover points — exactly where BagMonitor's real-time monitoring and alert system is designed to intervene. This is BagMonitor's primary product narrative.

## The Tracking Gap — and the Window BagMonitor Fills

The industry is rapidly accelerating toward real-time tracking, but infrastructure lags commitment.

| Metric | Current (2024) | By 2027 |
|---|---|---|
| Airlines offering real-time bag status to passengers | 42% | 82% |
| Airlines offering real-time bag status to ops teams | 43% | 92% |
| Airlines offering unassisted self-service bag drop | 66% | 82% |

**BagMonitor implication:** 58% of airlines do not yet offer passenger-facing real-time tracking. 57% do not yet have full ops-team tracking. The window to become the infrastructure provider for this wave of adoption is open now.

## Domestic vs. International — The Complexity Premium

| Flight Type | Mishandling Rate | 2024 vs 2023 |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic | 1.9 per 1,000 pax | –21% |
| International | 11.2 per 1,000 pax | –7% |

International baggage mishandling is nearly **6x higher** than domestic. The gap is driven by added complexity: longer layovers, tighter connections, multiple carrier handovers, and cross-border logistics. This is where BagMonitor delivers disproportionate value — international routes, transfer hubs, and multi-carrier itineraries are the highest-risk and highest-cost operational environments.

**Sales targeting implication:** Airlines with heavy international and hub-and-spoke operations should be prioritized in outbound sales. Low-cost domestic-focused carriers are a secondary segment.

## Recovery Speed — the Second Battleground

Prevention is the primary pitch. Recovery speed is the secondary one.

- **66%** of mishandled bags were returned within **48 hours** in 2024
- **5.41 million** bags returned within 12 hours
- **8.4 million** returned within 24 hours
- **8.3 million** returned within 24–48 hours

Regional recovery performance varies significantly, reflecting differences in data sharing maturity and system integration:

| Region | Bags returned within 48h |
|---|---|
| Europe | 4.4 million |
| Americas | 3.2 million |
| Asia-Pacific | 1.4 million |
| Middle East & Africa | 1.2 million |

**BagMonitor implication:** Fast recovery depends on data moving fluidly between operators. BagMonitor's event stream and API layer directly enables the kind of real-time coordination that accelerates recovery — a secondary but compelling value proposition when the primary prevention pitch needs to be complemented with operational resilience messaging.

## Passenger Expectations — the Business Case for Airlines

These figures help airlines internally justify the investment in baggage infrastructure.

- **47%** of passengers say mobile tracking would make them more confident checking in a bag
- **38%** say a digital ID tag would increase their trust
- **42%** of passengers already use real-time baggage tracking (where available)
- Passengers increasingly expect baggage visibility equivalent to food delivery or ride-hailing apps

**Framing for airline buyers:** Investing in BagMonitor is not just an operational decision — it is a customer retention and NPS decision. Passengers who experience transparent, proactive baggage communication are more likely to trust the airline with future checked bags, reducing carry-on pressure and increasing ancillary revenue.
