## IATA Resolution 753 & mishandling costs

IATA Resolution 753 was officially introduced in June 2018. It mandates that member airlines track bags at four key journey points: check-in, loading, transfer, and arrival.

Baggage mishandling is expensive: roughly **€100–$150 per bag** in direct costs, plus long-term brand reputation damage.

## Operational challenges at Aegean

- Infrastructure gaps: needed to implement tracking not only in modern mega-hubs but also on remote Greek islands with zero existing baggage infrastructure.
- Post-pandemic staff shortages and an influx of inexperienced new hires unfamiliar with legacy procedures.
- Internal resistance: had to constantly convince stakeholders of the resolution's mandatory nature.

## "Baggage 360" — replacing legacy systems

To address these gaps, Korosis mapped legacy processes to identify operational inefficiencies, then launched the **"Baggage 360"** project: a modern, intuitive UI replacing outdated legacy systems. The app freed agents from being confined behind a physical lost-and-found desk.

**Proof of impact:** During a BHS breakdown in Athens that stranded over 1,000 bags, just **two agents using the app reflighted 600 bags in three hours**. A task that normally would have taken days and disrupted the entire workforce.

## Leveraging BI to fix systemic issues

Aegean deployed Business Intelligence (BI) suites to analyze baggage flows in real-time. The data revealed a root cause: only six airports in Greece had customs authorities, forcing transfer passengers from outside the Schengen area to constantly re-check bags in Athens — causing massive mishandling rates at that hub.

Armed with quantitative data, Aegean successfully lobbied Greek authorities to **expand customs presence to more airports**, drastically reducing mishandling rates at the source rather than treating symptoms.
