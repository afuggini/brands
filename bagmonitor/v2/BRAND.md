# BRAND.md — BagMonitor v2

This document covers the **visual brand layer** for the v2 identity — personality, formula, references, and emotional target. It is a companion to `v2/DESIGN.md` (visual system) and inherits the full strategy, voice, personas, pillars, hard-no's, and competitive positioning from `../brand/BRAND.md`. Read both before producing any content or visual output.

---

## 1. What BagMonitor Is

**Baggage visibility infrastructure** — an enterprise B2B product for airlines, ground handlers, and airport operators. Not a gadget. Not a consumer app. The full positioning is in `../brand/BRAND.md §1–3`.

---

## 2. Visual Personality

### Keywords

Calm · Precise · Bright · Clean · Modern · Architectural · Technical · Confident · Human-centered · Operational

### Not

Aggressive · Futuristic · Dark · Military · Cyberpunk · Busy · Noisy

The brand is closer to Bloomberg Terminal, Ramp, Stripe, Linear, or Honeywell than to a travel startup. It should feel like the systems a major airline already trusts — not like a feature they have to be sold on.

---

## 3. The Formula

**Ramp + Swiss Editorial Design**

The result is: minimal, bright, premium, technical, calm, operational.

The feeling should be: *critical infrastructure made understandable.*

Neither half of the formula dominates. Ramp contributes directness, precision, and financial-grade credibility. Swiss editorial contributes structure, typographic clarity, and a timelessness that avoids tech-startup cycles.

---

## 4. Reference Brands

### Look toward

| Brand | What to borrow |
|---|---|
| Ramp | Precision, restraint, financial-grade credibility, clean type |
| Bloomberg | Data-forward, operator vocabulary, no consumer softening |
| Stripe | Developer trust, documentation clarity, considered whitespace |
| Linear | Craft, speed, dark-light flexibility, minimal chrome |
| Honeywell / Siemens | Infrastructure authority, calm confidence |
| Airport systems signage | Clear hierarchy, legibility under pressure, Swiss grid |

### Never look toward

- Travel startups or passenger-facing apps
- Vacation photography, smiling passengers, planes taking off
- Neon or cyberpunk technology aesthetics
- Generic SaaS "hero metric" templates
- Consumer tracker brands (AirTag, Tile, etc.)

---

## 5. Emotional Target

Users (operators, executives, integrators) should feel:

**Confidence · Clarity · Control · Trust**

Never: anxiety, urgency, complexity, or chaos.

The visual system should communicate *we make complex airport operations understandable* — not through explanation, but through the experience of looking at it.

---

## 6. Voice (inherited + restated)

Four characteristics inherited from `../brand/BRAND.md §6`:

1. **Confident** — declarative, no hedging. BagMonitor speaks from operational authority.
2. **Technical** — precise domain language; the audience (airline ops, CTOs) knows their own terms.
3. **Clear** — fewer words win. One claim per sentence. No buzzword stacking.
4. **Credibility-first** — data before claims. Mechanism before assertion.

### Copywriting rules specific to v2

- Avoid contrasting-negation patterns: "X isn't Y. It's Z." or "X is Y, not Z." Write direct affirmative statements.
- Avoid fragmented clauses joined by em dashes. Write full, flowing sentences.
- Avoid abusing short punchy pairs ("The bag moves. The data doesn't."). One is voice; five is a tic.
- Avoid colons as dramatic pivots ("The result: perfect tracking"). Weave into the sentence.

### Primary CTA

**Book a Demo** — all website and outreach activity drives toward a qualified demo request.
